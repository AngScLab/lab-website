The genetic basis of skin color across global populations is an age-old mystery. 
The dark skin of African populations, the ancestral state in humans, is protective 
in regions with high solar UV exposure. In contrast, the light skin of Europeans 
has a selective advantage in northerly latitudes due to higher sun-dependent 
generation of vitamin D.

European skin is associated with up to a 30-fold increased risk of melanoma,  
the deadliest form of skin cancer. Surprisingly, East Asians, although 
light-skinned, have very low risk of melanoma comparable to Africans. They also 
do not carry the pervasive European light skin color genes (*SLC24A5* & *SLC45A2*), 
meaning they must carry a functionally similar genetic variation.

We aim to identify the East Asian light skin color gene by studying populations 
with a combination of ancestral (African) and primary East Asian 
ancestries.
